# Slide-by-Slide Output Template

A blueprint for turning a case study into a presentation. Two sequences: a
standard project narrative, and a growth/experimentation variant. Both are
flexible — bend them to the story.

## Formatting principles
- **Headlines are statements, not labels.** "Cutting two taps doubled completion"
  beats "Onboarding redesign."
- **One idea per slide.** If you're cramming, split it.
- **Visuals do the heavy lifting; copy supports.** Don't put an image on every
  slide by reflex — each must serve a purpose.
- **No redundancy between spoken and written.** Speaker notes are conversational
  — what you'd actually say, not a script of the slide text.
- **Cut weak slides.** 10 strong slides beat 14 with filler.
- **End with a CTA** — what should the audience take away or do next?

Each slide = strategic headline + concise supporting copy + visual (with caption)
+ speaker notes.

## Standard sequence (most projects)
1. **Title** — project name, your role, one-line hook. That's it.
2. **Context** — the situation and why it mattered.
3. **Problem** — the specific problem, framed sharply.
4. **Discovery** — research, insights, what reframed the problem.
5. **Approach** — your POV and the direction you chose (and what you rejected).
6. **Solution** — the design walkthrough, a few key moments.
7. **Craft detail** — one slide on the disproportionate-effort moment.
8. **Impact** — outcomes with baselines.
9. **Reflection / CTA** — what you learned, what's next.

## Growth-design sequence (experiment-heavy work)
1. **Title** — project + headline metric.
2. **Impact up front** — the result, stated first.
3. **Context** — the surface and the goal.
4. **Insight** — the behavioral observation that drove the hypothesis.
5. **Constraints** — what bounded the experiment.
6. **Iteration cycles** — the tests, what each one taught.
7. **Validation** — how you knew it was real (cohorts, guardrails).
8. **Layered outcomes** — user behavior → product metric → business impact.
9. **What's next / CTA.**

Maps loosely to narrative frameworks: standard ≈ 6-part default; growth ≈ Pyramid
(answer first). See `../references/narrative-frameworks.md`.
