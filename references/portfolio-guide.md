# Portfolio-Level Guide

Use this when the user asks about their portfolio as a whole, not a single case
study.

## The shape of a strong portfolio
- **3–5 case studies, not 10.** Depth beats breadth.
- **Lead with the strongest.** Reviewers often read only one fully.
- **Vary project types.** Don't show five redesigns of the same thing.
- **Each case study feels editorially distinct.** Different structure, emphasis,
  tone — the portfolio itself should demonstrate range.
- **First impressions are fast.** The hero shot and first paragraph decide
  whether someone keeps reading.

## What hiring managers & design leads look for
1. Clear problem framing — can you state the problem in one sentence?
2. Evidence of process — did you think, or just make pretty screens?
3. Strategic reasoning — why this solution over alternatives?
4. Impact & outcomes — what changed because of your work?
5. Self-awareness — what would you do differently?
6. Communication clarity — followable in five minutes?

## Patterns from strong portfolios
- **Lead with a thesis** and dwell on the problem space before showing solutions.
- **Show the messy middle** — sketches, whiteboards, failed iterations.
- **Pair each decision with the insight that drove it.**
- **Concise writing**, clean hierarchy, lots of whitespace.
- **End on measurable impact** and a short, honest reflection.

## Universal principles
- **Lead with impact** — ✗ "I was tasked with redesigning checkout." ✓ "A 34%
  abandonment rate cost $2.1M/yr. I led the redesign that halved it."
- **Show thinking, not just output** — include explorations that didn't ship.
- **Be specific** — "9 of 12 users couldn't tell if their order went through."
- **Keep it scannable** — gist in 60s from headers + captions; full read ~5 min.
- **End strong** — quantify, include a real quote if you have one, reflect.

## Anti-patterns
- The screenshot dump — pretty screens, no thinking.
- The novel — 3,000+ words, no visual breaks.
- Missing your role — "we did X."
- No metrics — "it was a success" with no evidence.
- Process theater — a double-diamond diagram with no real artifacts.
- Burying the lede — 500 words of background first.
- Generic descriptions — "I iterated based on feedback."
