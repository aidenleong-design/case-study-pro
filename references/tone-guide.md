# Tone & Voice Guide

The case study should sound like the designer talking to a smart peer — not a
brochure, and not an AI. Voice is a primary signal reviewers read for.

## Voice rules

- **First person, past tense.** "I shipped," "I cut," "I learned."
- **Active verbs.** shipped, identified, cut, reframed, killed — not "was
  responsible for."
- **Lead with impact, not setup.** Open on the outcome or the question, not "I
  was tasked with…".
- **Short paragraphs (2–4 sentences).** Scannable. Vary sentence length.
- **Name tools and tech specifically.** Figma, Maze, PostHog, `translateY`.
- **Attribute team work honestly.** First person for your contribution; name
  what others did. Never "we" to blur ownership.
- **Code as seasoning only.** 5–15 line snippets, and only when they clarify.
- **Every visual earns its place.** Skip empty blocks.

## Banned phrases (AI-slop tells)

Avoid these constructions:
- Em-dashes used as sentence separators (use them sparingly, if at all).
- "It's not just X, it's Y."
- "Whether you're…" framing.
- "Let's dive in" / "let's dive deeper."
- Hedging openers: "It's worth noting," "Notably."

Avoid these words:
- delve, leverage, utilize, unlock, navigate (figurative), unpack (figurative),
  empower, streamline, robust, seamless, intuitive, "in order to."

## Calibration examples

**Lead with impact**
- ✗ "I was tasked with redesigning the checkout flow."
- ✓ "A 34% cart-abandonment rate was costing ~$2.1M a year. I led the redesign
  that cut it in half."

**Be specific**
- ✗ "I conducted user research and found several pain points."
- ✓ "I interviewed 12 users over two weeks. Nine of 12 hit the same wall: they
  couldn't tell if their order had actually gone through."

**Show thinking, not just output**
- ✗ "I iterated based on feedback."
- ✓ "Usability testing killed my tabbed layout — users never found tab two — so
  I collapsed it into a single scroll."

## Self-check before delivery

- Could any designer have written this, or is the POV unmistakable?
- Does it read as authored, or assembled?
- Is there a real opinion somewhere a reader might disagree with?
